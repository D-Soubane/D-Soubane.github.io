PHD THESIS OVERVIEW Title: Exploration of Synergy Between Experimental and Computational Approaches for Optimized Energy Harvesting in DSSC Cells Dye-Sensitized Solar Cells (DSSCs) represent a promising technology for harnessing solar energy due to their relatively low cost, flexibility, and ability to operate under low-light conditions. This PhD project aims to optimize DSSCs by combining theoretical and experimental approaches. By integrating DFT simulations conducted in Morocco, via Quantum Espresso, with experimental work in France focused on the synthesis and characterization of prototypes, our research establishes a direct link between computational predictions and the actual performance of the devices, with the goal of improving the efficiency of DSSCs.
